Hanbin Hu

I am currently a third-year master student in Electronics Science and Technology at Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU). My advisor is Prof. Guoyong Shi who leads the Mixed-Signal Design Automation (MSDA) Lab in the School of Microelectronics. I received my Bachelor of Science degree in Microelectronics Engineering with a minor in Applied Physics from SJTU in 2013.

My research interests mainly focus on Computer-Aided Design (CAD), Electronic Design Automation (EDA) and advanced circuit design techniques. I am fascinated by how software can facilitate circuit design from system level to physical level. I have joined MSDA Lab since 2012, and have conducted several research projects on symbolic analysis methods and model order reduction. As a result of my research, I have published two papers (ASICON-2013 and ISCAS-2015) and submitted one paper to ISCAS-2016. You can find more information about my research and projects in Projects. I also took internship twice at Synopsys, Shanghai in 2014 and 2015.

I grew up in Shanghai, China. Shanghai is an international metropolis nurtured by both traditional Chinese culture and foreign culture, full of archaistic narrow alleys as well as modern towering skyscrapers. I have lived here for nearly 25 years. I'd like to travel to see the big world.

The great passion and joy gained from my research experience in EDA have strongly encouraged me to pursue a PhD degree in the U.S. Hope one day I can make significant contribution to the community and my work will accelerate innovation in both academia and industry.
